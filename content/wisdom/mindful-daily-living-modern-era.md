   - What am I grateful for today?
   - What did I learn?
   - What could I do better tomorrow?
   - Who did I help? Who helped me?

   - Count breaths 1-10, repeat
   - If mind wanders, start over
   - No judgment, just return to breath

**Target: 7-8 hours of quality sleep**

## ğŸ² Mindful Eating in Modern Times

### Buddhist Approach to Food:

The Buddha taught moderation and mindfulness with food:

#### Five Contemplations Before Eating:

1. This food is the gift of the whole universe (earth, sky, rain, sun)
2. We are grateful for the work that brought this food to us
3. We receive this food with awareness
4. We eat only foods that nourish us and prevent illness
5. We accept this food to realize the path of understanding and love

### Modern Mindful Eating Guidelines:

- **Eat real food, mostly plants, not too much** (Buddhist-friendly diet)
- **Honor the precept** against harming (consider vegetarian/vegan options)
- **Eat when hungry, stop when 80% full** (hara hachi bu)
- **Avoid eating from stress, boredom, or emotion**
- **Prepare food with love and care**
- **Appreciate every bite**

#### Sample Daily Menu:

**Breakfast**: Oatmeal with fruits, nuts, green tea
**Lunch**: Quinoa bowl with vegetables, tofu, miso soup
**Dinner**: Brown rice, steamed vegetables, lentil curry
**Snacks**: Fresh fruit, nuts, herbal tea

## ğŸ› ï¸ Troubleshooting Common Modern Challenges

### "I don't have time for all this!"

**Buddha's Response**: You don't have time NOT to do this.

Start small:
- 5 minutes meditation
- 1 mindful meal per day
- 10-minute evening walk
- Phone-free breakfast

**Build gradually**. Even small practices transform lives.

### "My work demands are too high!"

**Consider**:
- Can you set better boundaries?
- Is this job aligned with your values?
- Are you caught in the "busy badge of honor"?
- What would you advise your best friend in this situation?

Sometimes we need to make hard choices for our wellbeing.

### "I keep falling off track!"

**This is normal**. The path isn't straight.

**When you stumble**:
1. Notice without judgment
2. Smile at your humanity
3. Return to your practice
4. Don't wait for Monday/January 1st
5. Start again right now

> "A jug fills drop by drop." - Buddha

### "I feel guilty for resting!"

**Buddhist truth**: Rest is not laziness. Rest is necessary.

- Rest restores your ability to serve
- Rest prevents burnout
- Rest honors your body's needs
- Rest is productive (your body heals and integrates)

**Permission granted**: You can rest without earning it.
