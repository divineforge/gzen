## Observation

I do not write when I feel like writing. I write at 6am, before the day has generated enough noise to produce an excuse.

On the mornings I feel like writing, the writing is the same as the mornings I do not. The feeling was irrelevant to the output.

## Principle

Motivation is a product of action, not a precondition for it.

We are taught to wait for the feeling — inspiration, energy, readiness — before we begin. But the feeling arrives, when it arrives, only after we have begun. It is a reward for starting, not a requirement for starting.

Discipline is the practice of acting without waiting for the feeling. It is not about forcing yourself. It is about removing the question of whether you feel like it from the decision entirely.

## Application

Establish a structure that does not require motivation to execute.

The structure should be so simple that you cannot negotiate with it. Not "write when inspired" but "write at 6am." Not "exercise when you have energy" but "exercise at 7am."

Remove the decision. What does not require a decision cannot be refused.

The feeling will come or it will not. Either way, the work gets done.
